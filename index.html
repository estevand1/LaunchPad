ARC treats college as one of five legitimate paths ‚Äî alongside trade programs, direct workforce entry, community college, and entrepreneurship. No path gets a hidden endorsement. Each one gets evaluated on the same criteria: income potential, cost, time to earning, and personal fit.

Page Section 7 ‚Äî Program Structure and Format

How the program works

Format

ARC runs over 6 weeks with two sessions per week (12 sessions total). Sessions are 60‚Äì75 minutes each and can be run one-on-one or in a small group of 3‚Äì6 students. Both formats are effective ‚Äî the small group option adds the benefit of hearing how peers navigate the same decisions.

What each session looks like

Every session has a specific goal, a structured agenda, and a worksheet or exercise the student completes in real time. There's no homework load ‚Äî the work happens in the sessions. What students take home is the output of the session itself: a filled-in worksheet, a completed roadmap section, a draft of their budget.

The binder

Every student builds a physical ARC binder over the course of the program. By Session 12, it contains every deliverable from every session ‚Äî a complete record of their work that doubles as a reference document they can return to. It's tangible in a way a Google Doc isn't.

Session 12: Graduation

The final session is a presentation and commitment ceremony. Students share their Action Blueprint out loud ‚Äî to the group, and optionally to a parent or family member ‚Äî and sign a personal commitment contract. It's a low-pressure but meaningful moment of accountability that makes the plan feel real, not theoretical.

Page Section 8 ‚Äî FAQ

Common questions

Does this push students toward college?

No. College is one of five paths we evaluate ‚Äî alongside trade programs, direct workforce entry, community college, and entrepreneurship. Every path is taken seriously. The goal is to find the right fit for each student, not to funnel everyone in the same direction.

What if my student doesn't know what they want at all?

That's exactly who this is designed for. The program starts by helping students understand how they work best ‚Äî before asking them to evaluate any options. Most students who "don't know what they want" actually have more self-knowledge than they realize. The first two weeks surface it.

What if the plan changes after the program ends?

Plans always change ‚Äî that's built into the program. Session 10 specifically teaches a decision-making framework for navigating pivots confidently. Students leave knowing how to adapt, not just what to do under ideal circumstances.

Is this therapy or counseling?

No. ARC is a structured coaching and planning program. It's practical and forward-looking. If a student is navigating significant mental health challenges or family instability, we'll have an honest conversation about whether ARC is the right fit right now and what might serve them better.

Can parents be involved?

Parents are welcome at the final session (Session 12) where students present their Action Blueprint. Outside of that, the sessions belong to the student ‚Äî which is intentional. Students engage more honestly and take more ownership when the sessions are their space. You'll see the results at graduation.

When should we enroll ‚Äî senior year, summer before, or after graduation?

The ideal window is second semester senior year or the summer before or after graduation. Early enough that the plan informs real decisions. Late enough that it doesn't compete with the chaos of college applications. Reach out and we'll help you find the right timing.
